Test 10.3

An LSI system has PSF $\displaystyle h[m,n]=\left[\matrix{1 & 2 & 1\cr 2 & \underline{4} & 2\cr 1 & 2 & 1}\right]. $ What is its wavenumber response? (HINT: $h[m,n]$ is separable.)




Solution:
By inspection, $h[m,n]=h[m]\;h[n]$ where $h[n]=\{1,\underline{2},1\}$. $h[n]$ has DTFT ${\bf H} (e^{j\Omega})=2+2\cos(\Omega)$.
$\displaystyle {\bf H} (e^{j\Omega_1},e^{j\Omega_2})=(2+2\cos(\Omega_1))(2+2\cos(\Omega_2)). $

Test 10.4

The 2-D discrete Laplacian has PSF $\displaystyle h[m,n]=\left[\matrix{0 & 1 & 0\cr 1 & \underline{-4} & 1\cr 0 & 1 & 0}\right]. $ Its wavenumber response is:




Solution:
$$ {\bf H} (e^{j\Omega_1},e^{j\Omega_2}) =e^{j\Omega_1}+e^{-j\Omega_1}+e^{j\Omega_2}+e^{-j\Omega_2}-4 =2\cos(\Omega_1)+2\cos(\Omega_2)-4. $$

Test 10.5

An LSI system has PSF $\displaystyle h[m,n]=\left[\matrix{1 & 1\cr 1 & 1}\right]. $ It could be used for:




Solution:
\begin{eqnarray*} {\bf H} (e^{j\Omega_1},e^{j\Omega_2}) &=& 1+e^{-j\Omega_1}+e^{-j\Omega_2}+e^{-j\Omega_1}e^{-j\Omega_2} \\ &=& e^{-j\Omega_1/2}2\cos\left(\frac{\Omega_1}{2}\right) +e^{-j\Omega_2}e^{-j\Omega_1/2}2\cos\left(\frac{\Omega_1}{2}\right) \\ &=& 2\cos\left(\frac{\Omega_1}{2}\right)2 \cos\left(\frac{\Omega_2}{2}\right)e^{-j\Omega_1/2}e^{-j\Omega_2/2}. \\ |{\bf H} (e^{j\Omega_1},e^{j\Omega_2})| &=& 4\left|\cos\left(\frac{\Omega_1}{2}\right)\cos\left(\frac{\Omega_2}{2}\right)\right|. \end{eqnarray*} This passes low wavenumbers and rejects high ones.

Test 10.6

An LSI system has PSF $\displaystyle h[m,n]=\left[\matrix{1 & -1\cr -1 & 1}\right]. $ It could be used for:




Solution:
\begin{eqnarray*} {\bf H} (e^{j\Omega_1},e^{j\Omega_2}) &=& 1-e^{-j\Omega_1}-e^{-j\Omega_2}+e^{-j\Omega_1}e^{-j\Omega_2} \\ &=& e^{-j\Omega_1/2}2j\sin\left(\frac{\Omega_1}{2}\right) -e^{-j\Omega_2}e^{-j\Omega_1/2}2j\sin\left(\frac{\Omega_1}{2}\right) \\ &=& 2j\sin\left(\frac{\Omega_1}{2}\right)2j\sin\left(\frac{\Omega_2}{2}\right) e^{-j\Omega_1/2}e^{-j\Omega_2/2}. \\ |{\bf H}(e^{j\Omega_1},e^{j\Omega_2})| &=& \left|4\sin\left(\frac{\Omega_1}{2}\right)\sin\left(\frac{\Omega_2}{2}\right)\right|. \end{eqnarray*} This passes high wavenumbers and rejects low ones.

Test 10.7

An LSI system has PSF $\displaystyle h[m,n]=\left[\matrix{1 & 0 & 1\cr 0 & \underline{0} & 0\cr 1 & 0 & 1}\right]. $ It could be used for:




Solution:
\begin{eqnarray*} {\bf H} (e^{j\Omega_1},e^{j\Omega_2}) &=& e^{j\Omega_1}e^{j\Omega_2}+e^{j\Omega_1}e^{-j\Omega_2} +e^{-j\Omega_1}e^{j\Omega_2}+e^{-j\Omega_1}e^{-j\Omega_2} \\ &=&(e^{j\Omega_1}+e^{-j\Omega_1})(e^{j\Omega_2} +e^{-j\Omega_2}) \\ &=& 2\cos(\Omega_1) 2\cos(\Omega_2). \end{eqnarray*} This is a bandreject filter, since it rejects midrange frequencies ($\Omega_i\approx\frac{\pi}{2}$).

(10.10), $$ h[m,n]**\;x[m,n] =\sum_{i=-\infty}^{\infty}\sum_{j=-\infty}^{\infty} x[i,j]\;h[m-i, n-j]. $$ $$ (1)\left[\matrix{1 & 2 & 0\cr 3 & 4 & 0\cr 0 & 0 & 0}\right]+(1)\left[\matrix{0 & 1 & 2\cr 0 & 3 & 4\cr 0 & 0 & 0}\right] +(1)\left[\matrix{0 & 0 & 0\cr 1 & 2 & 0\cr 3 & 4 & 0}\right]+(1)\left[\matrix{0 & 0 & 0\cr 0 & 1 & 2\cr 0 & 3 & 4}\right] =\left[\matrix{1 & 3 & 2\cr 4 & 10 & 6\cr 3 & 7 & 4}\right]. $$

Test 10.10

The $(2\times 2)$ 2-D DFT of $\left[\matrix{3 & 1\cr 4 & 5}\right]$ is:




Solution:
From Eq. (10.20a), $$ {\bf X} [k_1,k_2]=\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x[m,n]\;e^{-j2\pi(k_1m/M+k_2n/N)}. $$ The $(2\times 2)$ 2-D DFT of $\left[\matrix{a & b\cr c & d}\right]$ is \begin{eqnarray*} {\bf X} [k_1,k_2] &=& a+be^{-j\pi k_2}+ce^{-j\pi k_1}+de^{-j\pi(k_1+k_2)} \\ &=& \left[\matrix{a+b+c+d & a-b+c-d\cr a+b-c-d & a-b+c-d}\right]. \end{eqnarray*} The 2-D DFT of $\left[\matrix{3 & 1\cr 4 & 5}\right]$ is $\left[\matrix{13 & 1\cr -5 & 3}\right]$.

Test 10.13

Use the $(2\times 2)$ 2-D DFT to solve the cyclic deconvolution problem $\displaystyle y[m,n]=\left[\matrix{42 & 42\cr 28 & 31}\right]=h[m,n]\;©\; x[m,n]=\left[\matrix{3 & 1\cr 4 & 5}\right]\;©\; x[m,n]. $ Determine $x[m,n]$.




Solution:
The 2-D DFT is given by Eq. (10.20a), $$ {\bf X} [k_1,k_2]=\sum_{m=0}^{M-1}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}x[m,n] \;e^{-j2\pi(k_1m/M+k_2n/N)}. $$ The $(2\times 2)$ 2-D DFT of $\left[\matrix{a & b\cr c & d}\right]$ is $$ {\bf X} [k_1,k_2]=a+be^{-j\pi k_2}+ce^{-j\pi k_1}+de^{-j\pi(k_1+k_2)} =\left[\matrix{a+b+c+d & a-b+c-d\cr a+b-c-d & a-b+c-d}\right]. $$ The 2-D DFT of $\left[\matrix{42 & 42\cr 28 & 31}\right]$ is $\left[\matrix{143 & -3\cr 25 & 3}\right]$, the 2-D DFT of $\left[\matrix{3 & 1\cr 4 & 5}\right]$ is $\left[\matrix{13 & 1\cr -5 & 3}\right]$. Then $$ {\bf X} [k_1,k_2]=\frac{{\bf Y} [k_1,k_2]}{{\bf H} [k_1,k_2]} =\left[\matrix{11 & -3\cr -5 & 1}\right] $$ and, the 2-D inverse DFT is given by Eq. (10.20b) as $x[m,n]=\left[\matrix{1 & 2\cr 3 & 5}\right]$.

Test 10.14

Use the $(2\times 2)$ 2-D DFT to solve the cyclic deconvolution problem $\displaystyle y[m,n]=\left[\matrix{47 & 45\cr 30 & 32}\right] =h[m,n]\;©\; x[m,n] =\left[\matrix{3 & 1\cr 4 & 6}\right]\;©\;x[m,n]. $ Determine $x[m,n]$.

Test 10.19

Which scaling impulse response $g[n]$ satisfies the Smith-Barnwell condition?




Solution:
Smith-Barnwell condition: Autocorrelation of $g[n]$ is 0 for even $n\ne 0$. For $g[n]=\{a,b,c,d\}$: $ac+bd=0$ and $a^2+b^2+c^2+d^2=1$. Only ${\bf B}$ satisfes the former. See Section 10-11.4.
